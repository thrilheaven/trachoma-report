Executive summary

Many trachoma sufferers are mothers and children who unknowingly pass infection to each other with every hug or touch. Shashetu is a 25-year-old mother of two from Ethiopia. She makes a living weaving baskets and selling them at local markets. As the sole provider for her young family, she knows if she cannot see, she cannot work, and cannot feed her children. Trachoma was threatening to take away her livelihood.
