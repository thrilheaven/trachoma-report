Leaving no one behind

‘I thought I was going to go blind one day,’

For the past 6 years, 25-year-old Azmera has been in constant pain with trachoma. ‘I thought I was going to go blind one day,’ said Azmera who can see again after surgery. ‘I will now work and live my life and I can take care of my children better.’ Usually trachoma attacks the upper eyelid, but in Azmera’s case it had affected the lower lid.

‘I had a painful burning and prickling in both my eyes and lots of tears, as if I had something in my eyes all the time.’

Chief Abdul-Rahman had trichiasis in both eyes. He was gradually becoming blind, a state that would have been irreversible. He was told he could have an operation to end the pain. ‘I had a painful burning and prickling in both my eyes and lots of tears, as if I had something in my eyes all the time. I was really pleased to have found a solution. My vision is so much better now.’

‘Now I will be able to milk my two cows, fetch firewood and cook for my beautiful granddaughter.’

Adiyo wove and sold necklaces for eight months to pay for the transport to take her grandmother to the clinic. After the surgery, Cheposera sat up straight, beaming with delight. ‘Now I will be able to milk my two cows, fetch firewood and cook for my beautiful granddaughter.’

Sisters Awa and Arame Ndiaye each underwent fifteen minute operations for trichiasis on the same afternoon. Both sisters are widows and have found it hard to run their houses with limited eyesight and constant pain, but Awa said her eyes already felt better and she was looking forward to finding a new husband!
